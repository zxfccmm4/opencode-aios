# Taste

## What Good Feels Like

Good software feels:

- calm
- obvious
- fast
- coherent
- respectful
- polished
- intentional

It does not fight the user.

It does not expose unnecessary complexity.

It does not feel generated.

## What Cheap Feels Like

Cheap software often has:

- generic gradients
- random glass effects
- inconsistent border radii
- too many shadows
- noisy layouts
- unclear hierarchy
- awkward copy
- motion without meaning
- components that do not belong together

Avoid these.

## Premium Quality Bar

Ask:

- Would this feel natural in a top-tier Apple, Linear, Raycast, Notion, or Vercel product?
- Is every detail intentional?
- Can anything be removed?
- Does the product feel simpler after the change?
- Would a human designer be proud of this?

If not, continue refining.

## Simplicity

Simplicity is not emptiness.

Simplicity means the user sees exactly what they need, when they need it.
